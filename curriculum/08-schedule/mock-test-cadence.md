# Mock Test Cadence

Mocks are how you convert *knowledge* into *score under pressure*. Without them, you'll score lower in the real test than you expected.

## The cadence at a glance

```
Weeks  1-5  : No full mocks yet. Just topic-wise mini-mocks (20 Qs in 20 min).
Week   6    : First full TCS NQT-style mock (1 hour). Baseline score.
Weeks  7-12 : 1 full mock every Sunday (alternating patterns).
Weeks 13-18 : Same + 1 sectional mock midweek.
Weeks 19-24 : 1 full mock + 1 mock interview EACH Sunday.
```

## Mini-mock format (Weeks 1-5)

Every Sunday morning, 60 minutes, **same time** to mimic the real test:

```
20 min : 20 Quant Qs    (this week's topic + previous weeks)
20 min : 20 Reasoning Qs (this week's topic + previous)
20 min : 20 Verbal Qs    (vocab + grammar + RC short)
```

Score yourself. If a section drops below 60%, that's next week's "extra topic".

## Rules during the mock

1. **No phone. No water break. No music.** Like the real thing.
2. **Same time of day** as your placement test (most companies test 10 AM-1 PM).
3. **No googling answers mid-mock.** If you don't know, mark and move on.
4. **Finish even if you're failing.** That's the most important rule. Train yourself to finish.

## Post-mock review (1 hour, same evening)

This is where the magic happens. The mock alone isn't valuable — the **review** is.

```
1. Look at sectional scores. Which 2 sections were worst?
2. For each WRONG answer:
     - Did I misread the question?         (Reading speed problem.)
     - Did I forget the concept?           (Knowledge gap.)
     - Did I calculate wrong?              (Speed/accuracy problem.)
     - Did I run out of time?              (Pacing problem.)
3. Log the *category* in your error log. Patterns emerge in 2-3 mocks.
4. Pick 5 questions you got wrong; redo them tonight.
5. Pick 5 you got right but were unsure; learn the *right* method.
```

## Sectional / topic mocks (midweek, Weeks 13+)

A 30-min targeted mock on a single topic:

- "Time & Work — 25 Qs in 30 min."
- "Linked List — 5 problems in 60 min."
- "DBMS Theory — 30 MCQs in 30 min."

Use these to *kill specific weaknesses* surfaced by your weekly review.

## How to score yourself fairly

```
Section accuracy = (correct) / (attempted) × 100
Section coverage = (attempted) / (total) × 100
Section score    = correct / total × 100    ← what companies see
```

Target by Week 18: every section above 70%, total above 75%.

## Common scoring traps

- **Pseudo-good attempt rate**: attempting 90% but only 50% correct = bad. Better to attempt 70% with 90% accuracy.
- **Time crunch on coding**: many score 0 because they ran out of time on Problem 1. *Always read both problems first.*
- **English over-confidence**: even fluent students lose marks on RC by skim-reading.

## Useful free mock platforms

- **PrepInsta** (free + paid Prime) — closest to company patterns.
- **Faceprep** — good UI, sectional mocks.
- **TCS iON practice tests** (free) — official-ish.
- **AMCAT free demo** — for the AMCAT-style assessments (used by many small companies).
- **Naukri Campus / eLitmus practice** — for off-campus drives.

## The night before the real test

```
- Read the official syllabus on the company portal once more.
- Open the test platform; verify camera + mic + ID requirement.
- Sleep 7 hours.
- Don't open any new study material.
- Trust the prep — the mocks have done the work.
```

## Game-day morning

```
- 30 min: solve 5 easy aptitude Qs to warm up the brain.
- Eat a normal breakfast.
- Be at the test desk 20 min early.
- Open the test 5 min before time.
- Breathe. Smile. Start.
```

The mocks have **already prepared you**. The real test is just one more mock — except this one counts.
