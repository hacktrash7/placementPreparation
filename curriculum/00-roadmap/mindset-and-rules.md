# Mindset & Rules for Self-Learning

The hard part of this journey is **not** the syllabus — it's keeping yourself going alone for 6 months with no teacher reminding you. Read this carefully.

## 10 rules that decide your placement

1. **Show up every day.** Miss a day, never miss two. A 30-min day is still better than zero.
2. **Solve, don't read.** Watching a 1-hour DSA video and solving 0 problems = 0 progress. Inverse it.
3. **Write code with your hands, not by copying.** Even if you saw the solution, *close it* and type it again from memory.
4. **Time yourself.** Every aptitude set, every coding problem. Placement tests are speed games.
5. **Maintain an error log.** A single notebook (digital is fine) with: question I got wrong → why → correct method. Re-read every Sunday.
6. **Speak English out loud daily.** 10 minutes. Read a news article aloud, or narrate your day. Versant + HR rely on this.
7. **Don't chase too many resources.** 1 aptitude book + 1 DSA sheet + 1 SQL track + 1 mock platform is enough. Finish them.
8. **Sleep 7 hours.** Tired brain = bad aptitude scores. This is not optional.
9. **One mock every Sunday from Week 6.** Treat it like the real test. Phone off, no breaks, full duration.
10. **Build in public.** Push code to GitHub the same day you write it. Recruiters look at *recent* commits.

## How to learn a topic (the loop)

For *any* topic in this curriculum — aptitude, DSA, DBMS, anything:

```
1. Read / watch the concept (≤30 min). Don't binge.
2. Take notes in your OWN words. If you can't paraphrase it, you didn't understand it.
3. Solve 5 easy problems on it. Same day.
4. Sleep on it.
5. Next day: solve 5 medium problems on the same topic without notes.
6. After 1 week: redo 2 hard problems on the same topic — this is your real test.
7. After 1 month: appear in a mock. If you fail this topic, GOTO 1.
```

## Common traps to avoid

- **Tutorial hell:** Watching 20 hours of Python tutorials without writing code. Cap your watching at 30 min per topic, then code.
- **Resource hopping:** Switching books every week because the new one looks shinier. Finish one, then judge.
- **Skipping aptitude:** Many CSE students think "I'm a coder, I'll skip aptitude". You will fail TCS Quant in 15 minutes. Don't skip.
- **Doing only DSA:** Aptitude + English + Core CS together account for 60-70% of your eliminations. Balance daily.
- **Late-night cramming pre-test:** Don't. The night before a test → light revision, 7h sleep, sectional warm-up next morning.
- **Ignoring communication:** You can solve everything and still fail HR because you can't speak 2 minutes of clean English. Train this from Day 1.

## Tools you'll use daily (all free)

- **Code:** Python 3 + VS Code locally; LeetCode / HackerRank / GeeksforGeeks online.
- **Aptitude practice:** IndiaBix, PrepInsta, GFG Aptitude, Faceprep — pick one and stick to it.
- **Mocks:** PrepInsta PrepAI, TCS iON practice tests, Faceprep, AMCAT free demos.
- **Tracking:** Google Sheet with columns: Date | Apti Qs | DSA Qs | Topic | Hours | Notes link.
- **Notes:** Notion / Obsidian / a plain folder with markdown — your choice. Stay simple.
- **English:** BBC Learning English app, daily newspaper editorial (The Hindu / Indian Express).
