# Verbal Ability & English Communication

This module covers **two things** that students from average colleges often underrate:

1. **Verbal Ability** in the written test (grammar, vocab, RC, sentence correction).
2. **English Communication** — Versant test, GD, HR speaking, day-to-day fluency.

Many strong coders lose offers here. **Don't be one of them.**

## Part A — Written verbal section

### Topics

1. **Reading Comprehension (RC)** — 1 passage, 4-5 questions. Common in Infosys, Wipro, Cognizant.
2. **Sentence Correction / Error Spotting** — subject-verb agreement, tense, prepositions, articles.
3. **Sentence Completion / Fill in the Blanks** — vocabulary + collocation.
4. **Sentence Rearrangement (Para Jumbles)** — find the logical order.
5. **Synonyms & Antonyms** — vocabulary based.
6. **One-word Substitution** — "a person who studies stars" → astronomer.
7. **Idioms & Phrases** — common test favourites.
8. **Cloze Test** — passage with blanks (Infosys & Wipro use this).

### Grammar must-knows (one-line rules)

- **Subject-verb agreement**: singular subject → singular verb. Watch out for "one of the boys *is*", "neither A nor B *is/are*" (verb agrees with the noun closer to it).
- **Tenses**: when reporting past speech, push tense one step back ("She said she was tired").
- **Articles**: *a* before consonant sound, *an* before vowel sound, *the* for specific.
- **Prepositions**: collocate with verbs — *depend on, consist of, agree with (person)/to (proposal)/on (a point)*.
- **Modifiers**: place the modifier next to the word it modifies.
- **Parallelism**: items in a list must share the same form (verbs all -ing, or all infinitive).
- **Comparison degrees**: "than" with comparative; "the …est" or "the most …" with superlative.

### RC strategy

1. **Skim** the passage in 90 seconds — get the *gist* and structure.
2. Read **all 4-5 questions** quickly.
3. Answer **fact-based questions first** (line reference).
4. Tackle **inference questions last** (require re-reading).
5. *Eliminate* answer choices that introduce new information not in the passage.

### Vocabulary plan

- **5 new words a day**, every day, for 100 days. That's ~500 words — enough for any service-MNC test.
- Source: *Norman Lewis — Word Power Made Easy* (best for Indian students) OR daily editorial in The Hindu / Indian Express.
- For each word write: meaning + synonym + 1 sentence in your *own life*.
- Revise old words **every 7 days**. Use Anki / a simple flashcard app if you like.

## Part B — Spoken English & Communication (the silent score-killer)

Companies like Tech Mahindra (Versant), Accenture, Capgemini and Wipro increasingly weigh **spoken English**.

### Versant English Test — what it actually measures

The Versant test scores you on 4 sub-skills (0-80):

- **Sentence Mastery** — repeating sentences after hearing.
- **Vocabulary** — quick synonym/antonym in audio.
- **Fluency** — pace, pause control, smoothness.
- **Pronunciation** — clarity, intonation.

A score of **~55-58/80** is generally enough for Tech M / Capgemini cutoff.

### Daily 10-minute speaking routine (do this for 90 days)

```
Min 1-2 : Read aloud one paragraph from a news editorial. Record on phone.
Min 3-4 : Repeat 10 simple sentences (5-12 words) spoken at normal pace.
Min 5-6 : Describe a picture / photograph for 60 seconds non-stop.
Min 7-8 : Answer one HR-style question in 60 seconds (see prompts below).
Min 9-10: Listen to your recording. Note 3 issues (mispronounced word, long pauses, grammar slip).
```

### Prompts to drill (rotate randomly)

- Tell me about yourself.
- Why do you want to join an IT services company?
- Describe your final-year project.
- What's a challenge you've faced and overcome?
- What are your strengths and weaknesses?
- Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
- Tell me about a time you worked in a team and there was a disagreement.
- Why TCS / Infosys / Tech Mahindra specifically?

### Pronunciation tips

- **Don't speak fast** — clarity > speed. Versant detects mumbling, not vocabulary.
- **Stress the right syllable**: deVELop, photoGRAPHy, MANage, MANageMENT.
- **Indian English shortcuts to avoid**: skipping the soft "th" (replace "dis" → "this"), "v" vs "w" mix-up.
- **Pause at commas, full stop at periods.** That alone bumps fluency scores noticeably.

### Group Discussion (used by Tech M, Accenture, Wipro Elite occasionally)

- **Speak in the first 30 seconds** if you can — establishes presence.
- Use the **"Yes, and…"** pattern: acknowledge the previous speaker, then add a point.
- Avoid: shouting, monopolising, going off-topic, personal attacks.
- Have a **structure**: claim → evidence → example → conclusion.
- Common topics: "Is WFH the future?", "AI taking jobs", "Indian education system", current affairs of last 30 days.

### Essay writing (Tech M, Wipro, Capgemini)

- Usually 200-300 words in 20-25 min on a generic topic.
- Structure: **Intro (2-3 lines) → 2 body paragraphs (1 idea each, with example) → Conclusion (2 lines)**.
- Use linking words: *however, moreover, in contrast, for instance, therefore*.
- Practice **one essay every Saturday** from Week 4 onward. Topics in [`../09-resources/`](../09-resources/).
