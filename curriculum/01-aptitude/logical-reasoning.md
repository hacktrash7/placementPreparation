# Logical Reasoning

Pattern-finding puzzles. Very high return on effort — most students score >85% here with 30-40 hours of practice. Aim for **80%+ accuracy at ~60 sec/question**.

## Topic list (in suggested order)

### Group A — Pure pattern (Weeks 1-2)

1. **Number Series** — find the next/missing term. Try +, −, ×, ÷, squares, cubes, alternating series.
2. **Letter & Alphabet Series** — position values (A=1 … Z=26), reverse position (Z=1), gaps.
3. **Coding-Decoding** — letter shift, number substitution, mirror image.
4. **Analogies** — word:word, number:number, letter:letter pairs.
5. **Odd one out** — find the misfit in a set of 4-5.

### Group B — Logic & deduction (Weeks 2-4)

6. **Blood Relations** — family tree problems. Draw a diagram, don't keep it in head.
7. **Direction Sense** — compass + steps. Always draw on paper, mark N at top.
8. **Ranking & Order** — "A is 5th from left, 8th from right …" → total = sum − 1.
9. **Syllogisms** — Venn diagrams beat verbal logic. Practise "all/some/no" combinations.
10. **Statements & Conclusions / Assumptions** — read carefully, choose only what is *necessarily* true.
11. **Cause & Effect** — common in Wipro, Capgemini. Identify which event triggers which.

### Group C — Multi-step puzzles (Weeks 4-6)

12. **Seating Arrangement** — linear, circular, square. **Always draw**. Use elimination: "B is not adjacent to D" → eliminate combos.
13. **Floor / Box Puzzles** — 7-floor or 8-box arrangements with attributes.
14. **Data Sufficiency** — answer is *not* the value; it's whether the info is enough.
15. **Input-Output (machine logic)** — given step 1, predict step n. Look for sorting / shifting patterns.
16. **Cubes & Dice** — count painted/coloured faces; opposite face deduction.
17. **Logical Venn Diagrams** — overlap of 2 or 3 sets; pick the correct diagram.
18. **Clocks & Calendars** — also counted in quant; angle between hands, "day of week" trick (Zeller / 7-cycle).

## Universal tactics

### 1. Draw, always.

Sketch a diagram for any problem with relations, directions, or positions. Mental tracking is the #1 source of mistakes.

### 2. Eliminate, don't solve.

For MCQ puzzles, plug each option into the constraint and discard. Usually 2 options die immediately.

### 3. Use a "frame" for arrangement puzzles

```
   1   2   3   4   5   6   7   8
   _   _   _   _   _   _   _   _
```

Fill confirmed positions in pen; tentative ones in pencil/brackets.

### 4. Syllogism trick

Use 3-circle Venn templates:
- **All A are B** → A ⊆ B (A inside B).
- **No A is B** → disjoint circles.
- **Some A are B** → overlap.
- **Some A are not B** → A has a part outside B.

Test the conclusion *against the diagram*, not against your intuition.

### 5. Number series quick checklist

When stuck, test in order:
1. Common difference (AP)?
2. Common ratio (GP)?
3. Difference of differences (quadratic)?
4. Square / Cube pattern?
5. Sum of previous two (Fibonacci)?
6. Alternating two series interleaved?
7. Prime numbers in disguise?

## Daily drill template (45 min)

```
Block 1 (15 min): 10 questions on TODAY's topic — untimed, with workings shown.
Block 2 (15 min): 12 questions on TODAY's topic — timed (60 sec each).
Block 3 (15 min): 10 mixed questions on PREVIOUS topics — speed drill.
```

Log every wrong question with **why** you got it wrong:
- Misread the question?
- Wrong concept?
- Calculation slip?
- Ran out of time?

Look at the *why* counts weekly; that's where to invest.
